title: '"Please Introduce Yourself"'
description: "How do I best explain who I am?"
date: 2026-04-15
---

Recently, I attended a meetup where we were going through the usual round of introductions before the session. The intro round usually has a structure -- one informed by the host beforehand to limit the amount of tangents people go through. I, in all my undiagnosed-ADHD and time-blind might, was 15 minutes late to said session. This was partly because I started to look for _auto-rickshaws_ at peak timings, and had to actually **walk** to the venue.

As I sat down and huffed from exhaustion, I realized it was my turn to speak. I looked around, slightly dumbfounded, and managed to spit out my name and this website's address. I learned that I was supposed to give a hot take on writing. I forgot what "writing" meant as a verb and instead contributed a lukewarm take on "learning".

I don't think I'm a _bad_ public speaker -- I've ad-libbed tech puns on a stage before, and even got some laughs -- but defining myself is something I do much less of. I've been able to get away with it at college events; most other students only care about the clubs you're a part of or internships you've undertaken. But who am I outside of that? I've had 21 years to develop and drop many interests. How many of them do I still hold close?

These are some questions I ask myself as I work on [my about page](/whoami). At first it was funny to call it _/whoami_, but I can tell it's slowly losing its humour for me. And the irony makes me laugh again.

The current idea is to divide the question into facets: who am I as a college kid, an army brat, a software developer, or a chai lover? What brought me to these things, these interests? I'm afraid I've made the task more difficult for myself by distributing it even further. Difficult enough that I'm procrastinating working on my about page by writing a blog post about it. Man, websites are hard.

Alright then, that's enough for now. I'll get back to the self-introduction grind.
