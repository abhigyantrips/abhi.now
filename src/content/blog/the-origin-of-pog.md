I joined my college in September of 2022. It was a new campus; the "food court" was a white tent behind the hostel building, and the path from the hostel to the singular academic block was surrounded by blue metal sheets on both sides. There were cement trucks and towering cranes everywhere. The classrooms were so new that they still had the faint smell of paint. None of this has anything to do with the nickname.

![An example of the environment we'd walk in every day.](./images/the-origin-of-pog-01.jpg)

I met Rajat on my first day in class. Neither of us remember how, exactly. I told him I remember our first conversation being about building PC's and computer hardware. His response was, "I just remember you were sitting next to me, so I started talking." Man, I can't comprehend extroverts.

During lunchtime on the same day is when we ended up meeting Keshav and Satyajit and sat together at the mess. I had no idea back then -- as I ate _arhar dal_ and rice -- that I was sitting with four of my closest friends I'd have in college. This phenomenon needs to be studied.[^1]

It was one of these days in class, when our coordinator announced that he'd be selecting a new Class Representative (aka 'CR'). No one really wanted the responsibility, except this one guy who raised his hand to volunteer. I didn't really like this guy, being honest, and didn't want him to be in-charge of events related to our class.

I raised my hand out of spite and received a surprising cheer from the classroom. With more contestants participating, the decision came down to a vote -- which I won. This is what I would describe as my first "POG" activity.

> "POG", for those unaware, is an abbreviation that expands to "Play of the Game". It is often used in the live-streaming/gaming spheres when someone wants to highlight how well a play was executed.
>
> The executor of a "POG" action, then, may be deemed as "Pogger".

![Standing in front of the whiteboard of votes.](./images/the-origin-of-pog-02.jpg)

It must have been a few days after, when I met Raghav while having dinner with Shashaank. We were talking about something related to Discord bots and working with them, when Raghav joined in out of similar interest. We discussed for a while before he mentioned Disnake, a Python library he used for building bots.

Shashaank proudly mentioned that I was -- genuinely and coincidentally -- one of the maintainers[^2] of said library. The genuine randomness of this moment leaves me in awe[^3], because pretty much all my college projects that were to come in the next four years would be in a collaboration with Raghav. I would mark this as my second "POG" activity.

### Footnotes

[^1]: "Phenomenon" here is referring to how some of your closest friends were once strangers that just popped into your life. It probably has been studied, honestly. I'm open to receiving links!

[^2]: "Maintainer" might be a strong word since I mostly managed the website and Discord admin stuff, but I absorbed the comment with pride.

[^3]: Disnake was fairly small back then too; it started as a fork of the more popular Discord.py, so chances of Raghav knowing it were even lower.
