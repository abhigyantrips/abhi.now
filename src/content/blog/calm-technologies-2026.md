I have always felt that technology should be an enabler, not a subjugator. Other than the tinkerers who want it to be their full-time job, technology should help you and then stand back as you take in the human experience, naturally. I hope to write more on this in the future and have multiple articles I link back to.

In the meantime, though, my main gripe with how I interact with technology is how everything has _steps_. The drawback of having a rectangle that can do everything is that you have to go deep into the rectangle to find the specific thing you want to do in that moment.

I am reminded of this every time my mom is looking for a very specific setting on her phone, or suddenly learns that the Camera app can scan QR codes, or that holding the scrollbar can make you move through items faster. It's not even a generational thing, because my sister has had times where she's trying to open a document, but the "Word document" on one operating system holds a different file format than the "Word document" from another.
